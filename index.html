About

I'm Alexis — a computer scientist who has spent his career working on the parts of software that most developers never see.

I started in cancer genomics research at TGen, building the sequencing pipelines and mutation-detection tools that put numbers behind treatments. Then I spent several years at Xamarin and Microsoft working on the Mono runtime — the engine under the hood of cross-platform .NET. Most recently, I led the OS team at Wilderness Labs, where I built Meadow.OS from scratch: a full embedded operating system that lets .NET developers write firmware the same way they write apps.

I like the problems that sit at the boundary between hardware and software, between managed code and bare metal, between what's theoretically possible and what actually ships. If you have one of those, we should talk.

Process

How a typical engagement works.

  1. 01

    Discovery

    We start with a conversation. You explain the problem; I ask uncomfortable questions. By the end, we both have a clear picture of what's needed and whether I'm the right fit. No commitment required.

  2. 02

    Scope & Agreement

    I work project-based (fixed scope, fixed deliverables) or on retainer (ongoing access, monthly cadence). I'll propose the model that makes sense for your situation, with transparent pricing.

  3. 03

    Build & Communicate

    I work async by default with regular written updates. You always know where things stand. I prefer shipping working increments early over big-bang deliveries at the end.

  4. 04

    Handoff

    Code arrives with documentation your team can actually use. I'm available for questions after handoff and can stay on in an advisory capacity if the work needs it.
