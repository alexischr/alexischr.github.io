About

I'm a computer scientist with deep roots in runtime engineering, embedded systems, and computational biology. I love working at the intersection of low-level systems and developer experience — making hard things feel effortless for the people building on top.

My career has taken me from cancer genomics research at TGen, through .NET runtime development at Xamarin and Microsoft, to leading the operating system for Wilderness Labs' Meadow platform — a .NET IoT OS that lets mainstream developers build production-grade embedded devices.

I hold a B.S. in computer science from Arizona State University and have co-authored 41+ peer-reviewed publications in genomics and bioinformatics, cited by over 11,000 researchers.
